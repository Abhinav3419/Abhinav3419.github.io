The Indian Heart Is Different ‚Äî and Science Has Been Ignoring It

CVD claims 17.9 million lives annually worldwide (WHO). In India ‚Äî home to over 77 million diabetic adults ‚Äî coronary artery disease strikes nearly a decade earlier than in Western populations, driven by premature metabolic syndrome, genetic predisposition, and rapidly urbanising lifestyles.

The Indian cardiovascular phenotype features diffuse multi-vessel coronary involvement at younger ages, disproportionately high co-prevalence of diabetes and hypertension, and a markedly higher incidence of silent ischaemia ‚Äî where severe myocardial ischaemia progresses without patient awareness ‚Äî driven by diabetic autonomic neuropathy.

All established AI models ‚Äî Cleveland, Framingham ‚Äî were built on Western Caucasian cohorts. Their applicability to Indian clinical practice is fundamentally limited. I decided to fill that critical gap.

Background & Rationale

CVD accounts for an estimated 17.9 million deaths annually (WHO). In India ‚Äî home to over 77 million diabetic adults ‚Äî coronary artery disease manifests nearly a decade earlier than in Western populations, driven by a unique phenotype of premature metabolic syndrome, genetic predisposition, and rapidly urbanising lifestyles.

Despite this epidemiological reality, virtually all established ML prediction models ‚Äî including the widely cited Cleveland Heart Disease Dataset and the Framingham Heart Study ‚Äî were built on Western Caucasian cohorts. Their direct applicability to Indian clinical practice is fundamentally limited.

This project fills that critical gap: the first cardiac risk ML model grounded in authentic South Asian clinical ground truth, trained and validated on real India-sourced hospital data.

Research Methodology ‚Äî Five Phases
01
Individual Feature Analysis
13 Features
Each of the 13 clinical parameters ‚Äî spanning demographics, haemodynamic measurements, biochemistry panels, ECG indices, and advanced cardiac imaging ‚Äî is individually correlated with heart disease probability. Full pathophysiological justification is grounded in standard cardiology literature including Braunwald, Levine, Mattu & Brady, Agarwal, and Klabunde.
02
Pairwise Combination Analysis
78 Pairs
All 78 clinically meaningful feature pairs are systematically evaluated for synergistic predictive value. Pairs are categorised into six clinical domains: age-based, symptomatic, vascular, ECG, cardiac-functional, and metabolic. Each pair is ranked on a five-star combined correlation scale based on clinical literature support.
03
Triple Combination Analysis
286 Triplets
Priority triplets are assessed for emergent synergistic ‚Äî not merely additive ‚Äî predictive power. Highest-rated triplets span multiple diagnostic modalities. Key insight: certain three-feature combinations provide diagnostic certainty that no single feature or pair can achieve alone, revealing true clinical synergy in the Indian dataset.
04
SHAP-Guided Algorithmic Discovery
Higher-Order
Beyond triplets, SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) values and Random Forest feature importance scores guide higher-order combination discovery algorithmically ‚Äî replacing manual selection bias with statistically rigorous, reproducible feature engineering consistent with current best practices in medical AI research.
05
Clinical Validation Against Indian Outcomes
Real-World
All five ML models are evaluated against real Indian hospital outcomes under conditions of authentic clinical practice. Primary metric: AUC-ROC. Clinically weighted Recall (sensitivity) is prioritised ‚Äî in cardiac diagnosis, false negatives carry disproportionately high clinical cost. Every iteration ‚Äî successful and failed ‚Äî is documented with full transparency.
