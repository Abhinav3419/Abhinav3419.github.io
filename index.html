The Missing Variable
Cultural Calendar Features for Wedding Apparel Demand Forecasting in India

The Problem

India's $45 billion wedding market is driven by the Hindu Panchang calendar — families schedule weddings on Shubh Vivah Muhurat (auspicious) dates. A year with 85 muhurat days creates very different demand patterns than one with 52. Yet every retail demand forecasting system in India uses only Gregorian calendar features (month, quarter) and sales lags. No deployed system encodes the religious calendar that actually determines when weddings happen.

The Approach

I built a 16-feature cultural calendar layer encoding Hindu muhurat counts, inauspicious period flags (Pitru Paksha, Kharmas, Mal Maas), and Hijri calendar restricted days. These were tested against real Google Trends data (2010–2025) for "wedding lehenga," "sherwani," and "bridal saree" search volumes using Leave-One-Year-Out cross-validation across 14 annual folds.

Key Results

Ridge Regression: RMSE improved from 4.60 → 4.30 (+6.4%), p = 0.020, won 11/14 folds.

Gradient Boosting: RMSE improved from 4.51 → 4.23 (+6.3%), p = 0.018, won 10/14 folds.

Cultural features account for 31.8% of total predictive signal. hindu_muhurat_count ranks #4 among all features.

Why This Matters

  • First-of-its-kind: Google Scholar returns zero results for "muhurat + demand forecasting." This is genuinely novel applied ML research.
  • Deterministic and free: Muhurat dates for future years are calculable today — no data collection or API costs needed.
  • Cross-category applicability: The same feature layer applies to gold, jewellery, venues, catering, travel, real estate, and automotive.

E-Commerce Inventory Management

For platforms like Myntra, Ajio, and Nykaa Fashion:

  • Pre-season stock positioning: If November has 15 muhurat days (vs. avg 12), stock 20–25% more wedding inventory. Calculable 6+ months in advance.
  • Dynamic safety stock: A 6% RMSE improvement on INR 100 Cr wedding inventory = INR 3–5 Cr in reduced excess stock or avoided stockouts per season.
  • Proactive markdowns: Trigger clearance immediately after the last muhurat cluster — don't wait for lagging sales signals.
  • Seller communication: Share muhurat-based demand forecasts with marketplace sellers for better supply-side planning.
Every Indian e-commerce platform forecasts wedding demand, and none of them use the calendar system that determines when weddings happen. This study proves that omission is measurable and fixable. The features are free, deterministic, and ready to deploy.
