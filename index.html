The Journey

Applied mathematics meets machine learning — a foundation built on physics and first-principles thinking.

My approach to ML comes from a place most engineers don't start — applied mathematics and physics. The kind of first-principles thinking where you don't accept a result unless you can derive it from scratch. That rigour is now the backbone of every system I build.

I don't reach for AutoML and leaderboard chasing. I reach for physics-informed feature engineering, honest null results, and statistical rigour that would survive peer review. My M.Tech in Applied Mechanics from NIT Allahabad gave me thermodynamics, entropy, and oscillator theory — the exact toolkit that powers my ENSO prediction framework.

Today I build at the intersection of domain knowledge and ML systems: clinical AI that understands biomarker physiology, demand models that read the Hindu calendar, climate predictors grounded in free energy calculations, and product strategies rooted in science. I'm looking for teams that value depth over surface-level metrics.
